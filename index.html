Chapter 01

Their History

Triassic
252–201 MYA

The First Dinosaurs Emerge

After the largest mass extinction in Earth's history wiped out 96% of marine species, dinosaurs first appeared in the late Triassic period. Early dinosaurs like Eoraptor and Herrerasaurus were small, fast bipedal hunters. They shared the world with early crocodilians and large amphibians, competing fiercely for survival on the supercontinent Pangaea.

Jurassic
201–145 MYA

The Age of Giants

As Pangaea broke apart and lush forests spread across the planet, dinosaurs diversified explosively. This was the golden age of the great sauropods — Brachiosaurus, Diplodocus, and Apatosaurus grew to staggering sizes. Predators like Allosaurus hunted in the shadows of these giants. The first birds also appeared, evolving from small feathered theropod dinosaurs.

Cretaceous
145–66 MYA

Peak Dominance

The Cretaceous was dinosaur civilization at its most complex. Flowering plants transformed the landscape. Horned, armored, and crested dinosaurs thrived alongside the most iconic predators — Tyrannosaurus rex and Spinosaurus. Continents continued drifting, isolating dinosaur populations and driving enormous regional diversity across every continent.

K-Pg Event
66 MYA

The Great Extinction

A 10-kilometer asteroid struck the Yucatán Peninsula, releasing energy a billion times greater than an atomic bomb. The impact triggered wildfires, mega-tsunamis, and a years-long "impact winter" that blocked sunlight and collapsed food chains. Three-quarters of all species perished — including all non-avian dinosaurs. Only small, feathered dinosaurs survived: they became modern birds.

Their Evolution

Archosaur Origins

Dinosaurs descended from archosaurs — a group that also gave rise to crocodilians. Key early traits: an upright stance with legs directly beneath the body, unlike sprawling reptiles. This gave them a major speed and endurance advantage.

🍃
2
Major groups

The Great Split

Dinosaurs split early into two orders based on hip structure: Saurischia ("lizard-hipped"), which includes sauropods and theropods, and Ornithischia ("bird-hipped"), which includes all the horned, armored, and duck-billed dinosaurs.

🪶
160
MYA — First feathers

Feathers Evolve

Feathers didn't appear for flight — they evolved first for insulation and display. Fossils from China reveal that many theropod dinosaurs, including close relatives of T. rex, were covered in feathers. This discovery transformed our understanding of what dinosaurs looked like.

🐦
150
MYA — Archaeopteryx

The Bird Transition

Archaeopteryx, discovered in Germany, is one of the most important fossils ever found. A perfect transitional form — it had feathers and wishbone like a bird, but teeth and a bony tail like a dinosaur. It proved that birds are living dinosaurs, not just relatives.

📏
40m
Patagotitan's length

The Size Explosion

Sauropods achieved sizes no land animal has matched before or since. Patagotitan mayorum weighed up to 70 tonnes. Their secret: a bird-like respiratory system with air sacs throughout their bones, making the skeleton lightweight despite enormous bulk.

🧬
10K+
Living species today

Dinosaurs Survive

Birds are avian dinosaurs — a fact now universally accepted by paleontologists. When you see a sparrow or an eagle, you're watching a living dinosaur. With over 10,000 species, birds are the most species-rich class of land vertebrates on Earth today.

Late Cretaceous · 68–66 MYA
Tyrannosaurus Rex
"Tyrant Lizard King"

One of the largest land predators ever. Its bite force of 12,800 pounds per square inch could crush bone. Recent evidence suggests it may have had feathers in youth and excellent color vision.

"Three-Horned Face"

Velociraptor
"Swift Plunderer"

Far smaller than movies suggest — turkey-sized and covered in feathers. It used its famous sickle claw to pin prey, not slash it. Highly intelligent with a large brain-to-body ratio.

Early Cretaceous · 130–125 MYA
Spinosaurus
"Spine Lizard"

The largest predatory dinosaur known, even bigger than T. rex. It was semi-aquatic, with dense bones for buoyancy and a crocodile-like snout designed for catching fish in North African rivers.

Ankylosaurus
"Fused Lizard"
